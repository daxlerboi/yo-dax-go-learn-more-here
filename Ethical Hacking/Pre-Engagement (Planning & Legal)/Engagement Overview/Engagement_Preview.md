### 🔐 Stage 0.1 — Engagement Overview (Pre-Engagement Phase)

This is the **very first and one of the most critical steps** in any cybersecurity assessment or ethical hacking process.

---

## 🧭 What is “Engagement Overview”?

The **Engagement Overview** is where **both you (the tester)** and **the client (organization)** clearly define:

* 🎯 *Why the test is being done*
* 🧱 *What systems are involved*
* ⚖️ *What is allowed and NOT allowed*
* ⏱️ *How and when testing will happen*

Think of it like a **contract + strategy blueprint** before touching anything technical.

---

## 🧠 Why this stage is important

Without this step:

* You could accidentally perform **illegal actions**
* You might test **out-of-scope systems**
* You can **break production systems**
* Or even face **legal consequences**

👉 This stage protects **both sides** — legally and technically.

---

## 📋 Key Components of Engagement Overview

### 1. 🎯 Objectives

Defines the purpose of the test:

* Find vulnerabilities?
* Test defenses?
* Simulate a real attacker?

**Example:**

> “Assess security posture of web application against OWASP Top 10 vulnerabilities”

---

### 2. 🧱 Scope Definition

Clearly states what you can test:

**In Scope:**

* Domains (e.g., example.com)
* IP ranges
* APIs
* Mobile apps

**Out of Scope:**

* Third-party services
* Production database (sometimes)
* Employee personal devices

👉 This is **SUPER important** — never go outside scope.

---

### 3. ⚙️ Testing Type

What kind of engagement?

* Black Box (no info given)
* White Box (full access)
* Grey Box (partial access)

---

### 4. ⏱️ Timeline

Defines:

* Start date
* End date
* Testing hours (important to avoid downtime)

---

### 5. ⚖️ Rules of Engagement (RoE)

The “laws” of the test.

Includes:

* No DoS attacks?
* No social engineering?
* No data destruction?

---

### 6. 📞 Communication Plan

Defines:

* Who to contact if something breaks
* Emergency escalation
* Reporting frequency

---

### 7. 📄 Legal Authorization

This is **non-negotiable**.

Includes:

* Signed permission
* NDA (Non-Disclosure Agreement)
* Liability clauses

👉 Without this = **illegal hacking**

---

## 🔥 Real-World Analogy

Imagine you're hired to test a bank’s security:

* Engagement Overview =
  “You can test only the ATM system, not internal servers, not customers, not employees, and only between 10 PM–4 AM.”

Without that clarity… you’re basically a criminal, not a tester.

## 🧾 How you can write it in your `.md`

You can structure it like this:

```md
## 0.1 Engagement Overview

### Objective
Define the purpose of the assessment.

### Scope
List in-scope and out-of-scope assets.

### Testing Type
Black Box / White Box / Grey Box

### Timeline
Start and end dates of engagement.

### Rules of Engagement
Define allowed and restricted actions.

### Communication Plan
Contact details and escalation process.

### Legal Authorization
Signed agreement and NDA.
```
